The Agrotech Challenge

In precision Malaysian agriculture, timing is everything. Harumanis mangoes (Mangifera indica cv. Harumanis), primarily grown in Perlis, follow a sigmoidal (S-shaped) growth pattern from fruit set to harvest (approx. 12 weeks).

While knowing the cumulative weight $f(x)$ tells us the physical size, calculating the first derivative $f'(x)$ (the instantaneous rate of weight gain) allows agrotechnologists to pinpoint the exact peak metabolic window. This is when the crop requires critical potassium supplementation, precise irrigation, and pesticide bagging.

The Growth Model

Logistic Model
Cumulative Fruit Weight: $f(x)$
f(x) = L / (1 + e-k(x - x₀))
L (450g): Max target weight
k (0.6): Growth rate constant
x₀ (6 wks): Midpoint of growth
Growth Velocity (Derivative): $f'(x)$
f'(x) = k · f(x) · (1 - f(x)/L)

This beautiful derivative represents how rapid growth slows down as the mango approaches its maximum carrying capacity ($L$), showcasing resource limitations within the plant tissue.
